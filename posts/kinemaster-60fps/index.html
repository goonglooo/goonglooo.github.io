Are you experiencing difficulties when inserting a video layer and saving it to a higher frame rate in Kinemaster? Don’t worry, in this article, we will guide you through the process of adjusting the settings in Kinemaster to enable you to save videos in 4K 60fps. Follow along with the step-by-step instructions provided below.

Introduction to Kinemaster#

Kinemaster is a popular video editing app that allows users to create professional-quality videos on their smartphones. However, by default, the app limits the frame rate capabilities. But with a few adjustments in the settings, you can unlock the full potential of the Kinemaster app and save videos in stunning 4K 60fps.

Step 1: Open Kinemaster and Create a New Project#

To begin, open the Kinemaster app on your smartphone. Once launched, create a new project with a size ratio of 16 to 9. This size ratio is commonly used and provides a widescreen aspect ratio, perfect for most video projects.

Step 2: Insert the Video File#

After creating a new project, it’s time to insert your video file. Click on the insert button and choose the desired file from your device’s storage. Select the video file you wish to work with and proceed to the next step.

Step 3: Add Video Layer and Perform Analysis#

If you encounter an issue when inserting a video layer and saving it to a higher frame rate, follow these steps. Click on the three dots located at the top-right corner of the app. Please note that the dots may be covered by the Kinemaster watermark, so you might need to move it aside. Once clicked, a drop-down menu will appear. Select the “Run Analysis Now” option.

Step 4: Advanced and Experimental Settings#

While the analysis process is running, it’s important not to close Kinemaster or switch to another application. Wait until the analysis is completed.

After the analysis, navigate to the advanced and experimental settings. Click on the corresponding button to access these settings.

Step 5: Adjust Video Settings#

In the advanced and experimental settings, you will find several options. Here, we need to make the following adjustments:

  • Select “Allow Storage Up to 60fps” to enable higher frame rate saving.
  • Activate the addition of video clips up to 240 vs.
  • Select the “Video Layer Model Unlimited” option.

Once you have made these adjustments, check if all the options are now marked in red. This indicates that the settings have been successfully activated.

Step 6: Resume Video Editing#

Now that all the necessary settings have been adjusted, you can resume your video editing process. Prior to the settings adjustment, the maximum frame rate that could be saved was only 30fps. However, with the changes made, you can now save your videos in up to 60fps, providing a smoother and more fluid viewing experience.

Conclusion#

In conclusion, by following these simple steps, you can unlock the full potential of the Kinemaster app and save videos in impressive 4K 60fps. Whether you are a professional videographer or simply enjoy creating high-quality videos, the added frame rate capability will enhance your creations.
